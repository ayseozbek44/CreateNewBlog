What is the formula for happiness in business life?

“Working hard + Promotion = Being happy” This is how many people define the formula of happiness in business life and think that they will be happy after achieving success. However, on the contrary, happy people can be successful.

"What is the formula for happiness in business life?" If we ask, many people can answer the following: “Working hard / Doing good work + Being promoted = Being happy” However, many studies conducted in recent years refute this general view. Accordingly, it does not bring happiness to work hard, to do good jobs and to rise in career as a result. On the contrary, happiness brings success; happy people can be successful. Neil Pasricha, the author of The Book of Awesome and The Happiness Equation, says TED speaker, spending 20 minutes each day and applying the four items below makes happiness, and happiness brings success.

Walk three days a weeks

Physical activity has a huge impact on our mood. As a matter of fact, the research conducted by the researchers of Pennsylvania State University proves this. According to the study, people who take 20-minute walks three times a week score better in the happiness ranking than those who use antidepressants and those who use both walking and antidepressants. Therefore, by taking 20-minute walks three times a week, you can feel better and be happy.

Review your day

Neil Pasricha says that the positive situations and developments experienced during the day can be remembered again at the end of each day and be happy. Accordingly, at the end of each day, be sure to review the positive developments or experiences you experienced that day, and even write it down in a notebook if possible. This action will allow you to close your day with positive thoughts, so you will feel better. Pasricha states that writing down his positive experiences and reading them afterwards increases the happiness effect three times more and this action should be done weekly. On the other hand, you can feel happier every weekend by looking at the five positive experiences or developments you have experienced during the week.

Do good

Pasricha states that the five random favors you do in a week will make one even happier than exercise. These favors can consist of simple favors like holding someone's door or buying someone a cup of coffee. Pasricha points out that doing good makes one feel good and says, "When I hold the door for someone, I feel good because I'm proud of myself and it makes me happy."

Meditate

Pasricha emphasizes the importance of meditating for only 20 minutes and says, "Close your eyes, sit quietly and breathe deeply." Pasricha states that resting for 20 minutes in this way prevents distraction and meditation helps to work more efficiently.
